Welcome to my website

An ongoing project to showcase my professional and academic experiences, as well as my personal interests

Background

Hometown

I was born in the city of Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia. Growing up in an immensely diverse country, I lived and learned with friends and colleagues of varied ethnicities through primary school till college.

As a kid, I learned to speak 3 languages: Malay, Mandarin, and English, which was not uncommon among Malaysians. We were required to learn our national language (Malay) and English was the medium used to teach Science and Math classes. I learned to speak Mandarin from my parents and mostly at my public Chinese primary school - SJK (C) Khe Beng, while I continued learning the language through my secondary education at SMK Kota Kemuning, a public secondary school.

Family

Born to a mechanical engineer and a mathematics teacher, my siblings and I were very fortunate to be able to visit many parts of the world. Our family members enjoy a close relationship with each other even though we are miles apart.

Interests

I grew up without a great deal of electronic entertainment; I spent most of my time playing the piano, reading, or playing outdoors. Those activities cultivated my interests today; I'm a huge fan of classical music and sports, especially football (soccer).

As for TV programs, I only had access to documentary channels like the Discovery Channel and the National Geographic, which cultivated my interest in science and engineering from an early age.

University of Texas at Austin

Major

I started my graduate studies at the University of Texas at Austin in Fall 2016, enrolled in a PhD program under the Mechanical Engineering department. My decision to further my studies beyond a bachelor's degree was motivated by the demand of engineers with advanced degrees in the robotics industry, an industry I was very much interested in building a career in.

Having experienced the research scene as an undergraduate, I wanted to pursue a career in research and development, hence my interest in getting a PhD instead of just a master's. However, with my limited access to funding in robotics research, discontent of my lack of work experience, as well the ending of my research funding (of a computational materials project), I decided to graduate with a Master of Science in Mechanical Engineering in Spring 2018.

Courses

I encountered quantum mechanics for the first time when I took a course on mathematical modeling. It was one of the most intriguing subjects that I've encountered, and piqued my interest so much that I did my master's thesis in computational models of doped carbon nanotubes.

The course I valued the most in my master's degree is the one that I studied about optimal controllers. I've always had a great deal of interest in control systems theory, so learning how to design linear quadratic regulators and observers was very exciting.

I also had the opportunity to explore the field of machine learning. Motivated by a famous machine learning course on Coursera, I worked on a semester-long project to build an Ant Colony Optimization algorithm to study its deterministic properties.

Activities

In my second year, I worked as a Graduate Resarch Assistant under Dr. Eric Fahrenthold, investigating doping effects on the conductance of carbon nanotubes. Using resources provided by TACC I built computational models of carbon nanotubes to simulate and study its electronic properties.

I was also fortunate to make some great friends through playing soccer, and have participated in UT's intramural soccer in all the semesters I was here, winning three outdoor soccer and one futsal championship with my team Soccer Kids.

University of Michigan

Major

I transferred to the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor from Sunway University in Malaysia during Fall 2013 as a Mechanical Engineering major.

I chose Mechanical Engineering because I love the versatility of mechanical engineers in different fields of work, and I especially love performing hands-on tasks. Besides, my dad's experience and problem solving abilities as a mechanical engineer influenced my decision to pursue my studies in that field.

I graduated with a Bachelor of Science in Engineering (Mechanical Engineering) and a Minor in Music in Spring 2016.

Courses

At the University of Michigan, I took many interesting and exciting courses taught by instructive faculty members. The design courses offered by the Mechanical Engineering department were especially educational and fun. For my junior design project, my team designed a robotic linkage to deflect laser beams. This course was one of many that I took that intensified my interest for controls and robotic systems as I aimed to familiarize myself with that field of work.

I have also been fascinated by the music courses that I've taken in fulfilling my Music minor requirements; they provided a unique and an intriguing lens to music understanding and deepened my love for classical music.

Activities

In my first year, I joined the University of Michigan Solar Car team and designed a parking brake system for the car, which ultimately went on to win the 2014 American Solar Challenge. Afterwards, I was part of the Robotics and Motion Laboratory (RAMlab) for almost 2 years, working with a PhD student Wyatt Felt in developing a novel sensor that allowed feedback controllable soft robotic actuators.

Besides activities that were related to my major, I was also involved with the University of Michigan Malaysian Student Association (MiMSA), from participating in plays and performances to hosting regional sporting events.

Sunway University

Major

I started my American Degree Transfer Program (ADTP) in the 2011 Fall term. The program was designed to prepare students to transfer to American universities by providing courses to fulfill course requirements.

Driven by my fascination in scientific and technological development, I enrolled as a prospective Engineering student and spent 2 years in the program before transferring to the University of Michigan. I love building stuff and I wanted to be someone who could build machines and structures to for our society.

Courses

At Sunway University, I took fundamental courses that were mandatory for transfers to universities in the United States. Other than the required mathematics courses, the program also provided elementary engineering courses, which were Statics, Dynamics, and Thermodynamics; my first of the batch was Statics, in which I learned about structures and rigid bodies that were in equilibrium. It was also through these Engineering courses that I discovered the different fields in which a mechanical engineer could work in.

I also fulfilled my humanities requirement at Sunway University. One of the most memorable courses that I took was THEA 1000: Introduction to Theatre. For the entire semester, the students in the class assumed roles in a production company, and had a mass production by the end of the semester: my role in the 'company' was an actor, a role I enjoyed immensely.

Activities

During my time at Sunway University, I was involved with extracurricular activities regularly. For 1.5 years, I was part of the ADTP Student Committee as a Head of Human Resources and a Logistics team member. The projects involved were mainly event organizing, such as annual dinners, sporting events, freshmen welcome parties and so on.

I was also the main organizer in arranging an orphanage class trip, in which we donated all of our profits obtained from booths we opened at a university event. We went to the orphanage and helped clean up the orphanage house as well as bought them food and daily supplies.
